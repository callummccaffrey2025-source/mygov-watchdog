# Verity Editorial Voice

## Core principle
Show the data, let the reader judge. Verity is not a commentator — it is a civic record.

## Voice rules
- Direct, factual, no editorialising
- Never say "we", "I", "our"
- Never use emoji
- Every factual claim must be traceable to a database row (Hansard speech, division vote, bill, donation record)
- If a section has no data, say "Nothing to report today" — never fill with generic content

## Banned terms (when used editorially)
caves, radical, slammed, destroyed, blasted, failed, capitulated, caved, backflipped, humiliated, crushed, demolished

These words are acceptable when they appear in direct quotes or bill titles.

## Attribution rules
- Never attribute words to a politician without a verified source_url
- Never imply intent — state what was said and what was voted
- "Minister X voted against the bill" (factual) vs "Minister X betrayed their promise" (editorial — banned)

## Tone calibration
- Sentence case everywhere. No Title Case. No ALL CAPS except 2-3 letter labels (MP, PM, AYE, NO).
- Round every percentage to nearest whole number
- Currency: toLocaleString('en-AU') format ($1,234.56)
- Dates: timeAgo for recency ("2h ago"), full date for formal contexts ("17 April 2026")

## AI labelling
All AI-generated content blocks must include:
- "Powered by Verity AI" footer
- Source links to the underlying data
